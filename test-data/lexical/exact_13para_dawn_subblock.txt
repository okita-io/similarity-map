Mara entered silently, curtseying low before approaching Rhyannon. "My lady," she murmured, "I've done as you asked - the library is secure for your use."

Rhyannon nodded absently, her mind elsewhere. She needed answers, and she knew just where to start looking.

"I'm going there now," she announced abruptly, tucking the parchment into a hidden pocket in her skirt. "I'll need you to keep watch at my door - make sure no one disturbs me."

Mara curtseyed again before slipping out of the room as quietly as she had entered.

Rhyannon made her way down the winding stone staircase, each step echoing softly against the cold walls. She paused outside Baldwin's study, listening intently for any sign that he might still be inside. Satisfied by the silence beyond the door, she slipped inside and crossed to his desk, running her fingers along its polished surface.

She knew Baldwin kept meticulous records of their family history - perhaps something in those old tomes would reveal the truth about Elara's claims.

Rhyannon began pulling books from the shelves, stacking them haphazardly on a nearby table. She flipped through yellowed pages filled with spidery script and ancient diagrams, her eyes scanning for any mention of Baldwin or his former betrothed.

As she worked, Rhyannon's mind raced. If Elara was indeed after revenge, what did that mean for their future together? Could she trust anything Baldwin told her from now on?

She paused as a heavy leather-bound volume caught her eye. The title was embossed in gold leaf: "Bloodlines of the Noble Houses." With trembling fingers, Rhyannon opened it to the chapter on Hawkespear.

There, listed beneath Baldwin's name, was an entry she had never seen before:

"Baldwin Hawkespear - betrothed to Elara Oakshade until her death in 1527."

Rhyannon felt as though all the air had been knocked from her lungs. She sank back into the chair, staring at the page in disbelief.

Elara hadn't lied after all... but if she was still alive, where had she been all this time? And why was Baldwin keeping such a monumental secret?
